Important Mission

Apollo 11 – First Moon Landing

Apollo 11 was the first space mission to land humans on the Moon. It was launched by NASA on July 16,

1969, and made history when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin stepped onto the lunar surface on July 20,1969.

The Crew

  • Neil Armstrong – Mission Commander (first person on the Moon)
  • Buzz Aldrin – Lunar Module Pilot (second person on the Moon)
  • Michael Collins – Command Module Pilot (orbited the Moon while Armstrong and Aldrin explored)

Why it matters:

Apollo 11 was a huge milestone in human space exploration and proved that landing on the Moon —

something once thought impossible — was achievable through science, teamwork, and determination.
